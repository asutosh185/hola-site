With the advent of 2018 around the corner, it is time to plan for the new strategies for digital marketing. The plans and strategies successful in 2017 may not be relevant in 2018. Let’s see what creates a high impact on your customers and your business in 2018.

MicroMoments: Micro moments occur when someone turn to their smart phone to do something specific. It could be to do some research, to find a location or to buy something. In these micromoments, the consumers make decision and form preferences. These are the highly critical touch points where brands can impact the customer decision. Most of the time these micro moments occurs when people are travelling, waiting in the queue or visiting a store to make a purchase. In 2018, the successful brands will have the strategy to understand and meet the consumer needs in these micro moments.

Visualization: Emphasis on visualization remains the same as in 2017. If we revisit the past statistics, 59 percent of executives would rather watch video than read text while four times as many consumers would rather watch a video about a product then read about it. Seventy-four percent of millennial find video helpful when comparison-shopping, 60 percent prefer to watch a video to reading a newsletter.



Consumers are able to relate and engage to the visual content better than the text. Visuals will help you drive more leads causing higher sales and thus contributing to your bottom line.

Chatbots: A service run by artificial intelligence and it mimics human while interacting with the customer over a chat interface. See how chatbots are going to change the game in the next few years:

  • Chatbots are expected to increase cost-savings in billions of dollars a year in ecommerce, banking and healthcare.
  • Chatbot interaction is expected to increase from just 20% in 2017 to 93% in 2022.
  • Businesses are increasing their interactivity with other businesses within a chatbot experience.
  • In 2018, it’s estimated that 30% of interactions with tech will be conversation-based and 2018 is shoring up to be the “year of the voicebot.”


Smart Content: With the advent of 2018, generic content will be the thing of the past. The content personalized as to the consumer’s need will be creating demand i.e the content that can be tailored and delivered according to the prospect’s buying cycle stage. With the customer becoming more demanding and the rise of the competition, one size fit all theory will not work anymore. To be able to get more customer attention and engagement, smart content is the only answer. A research conducted by Hubspot shows, calls-to-action targeted to the user performed 42% better than calls-to-action that were generic. It is worthwhile spending time and energy to make the existing content smarter or creating a new generation of intelligent content.

Geo-targeting/Geolocation Marketing: Next thing to look out for in 2018 is Geo-location targeting and marketing. It is another form of personalized marketing which is tailored according to the location of the prospects. Based on the user location, only the local ads can be displayed. In Geo-location marketing, the brands can target mobile users. The customized push notification about an attractive offer can be sent as soon as they are in the nearby area.
